1 · The service

Enclave (operated by Enclave Host, Inc., “we”) lets you deploy WebAssembly apps to confidential-computing hardware, paid for per unit of runtime. Deployments are records on public smart contracts on the Base network; independent enclave machines claim and run them. By creating, funding, or operating a deployment - from this site, the CLI, or the API - you agree to these terms.

2 · Payments are crypto-only

All payments for runtime are made exclusively in cryptocurrency: USDC or ETH on the Base network, sent from a wallet you control. We do not accept fiat payment for deployments, and there are no invoices, subscriptions, or stored payment methods. Where the site offers a way to purchase USDC with a card, that purchase is a separate service provided by a third-party on-ramp under its own terms - it is not a payment to us.

3 · Payments are non-custodial

You pay directly from your own wallet by signing on-chain transactions. We never hold your funds and never have access to your keys. Funding a deployment forwards your payment in the same transaction; nothing is escrowed or held on your behalf. The “balance” a deployment shows is prepaid runtime - an accounting number on a public smart contract - not money in an account.

4 · Payments are final

Blockchain transactions are irreversible, and so are payments here: all payments are final. There are no refunds, chargebacks, or reversals, and prepaid runtime cannot be converted back into funds. Runtime you have paid for is consumed as your deployment runs; if a runner fails mid-lease, up to one lease interval of prepaid time can be lost. Double-check the app, the shares, and the amount before you sign - the chain will not give it back.

The failover design lets another enclave take over a funded deployment, but we do not promise that one will be available, that takeover will be timely, or that your app will run continuously. Do not run anything here whose downtime you cannot tolerate, and keep your own copies of anything you cannot afford to lose.
